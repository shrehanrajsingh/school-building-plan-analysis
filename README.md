BGA School Building Project
===========================

BGA School Building Project is the architectural design documentation for a
G+2 educational campus developed as part of the Civil Engineering Society
(CES) coursework by Group 3.  The project covers campus master planning,
floor-by-floor spatial design, structural and material specifications, safety
and sustainability features, and 3D visualization of the BGA School from
Nursery through Class XII.

This repository contains the original design deliverables (CAD drawings,
Blender models, presentation deck, walkthrough videos) and the final
architectural design report (Report.pdf).

Overview
--------

The BGA School Building Project is a meticulously planned G+2 educational
campus designed to support academic excellence, extracurricular development,
and safe, efficient campus operations.  The building uses a doubly loaded
corridor typology organized around a central courtyard that brings natural
light and ventilation into interior circulation spaces while hosting a
badminton court at +300 mm elevation.

Academic zones are distributed vertically by age group:

| Floor        | Occupancy                                              |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Ground floor | Nursery through Class VIII, administration, courtyard  |
| First floor  | Class IX through XII by section and academic stream    |
| Second floor | Science labs, library, computer lab, music room, etc.  |

The campus integrates cricket, football, basketball, and badminton facilities,
segregated parking for school buses, staff, and visitors, and landscaped
green areas throughout the site.

Building Program
----------------

The campus is organized into three functional zones:

**Academic Zone.**
Nursery through Class XII classrooms (7 m x 7 m each), science laboratories,
computer laboratory, library, music room, and seminar/conference facilities
distributed across three floors.

**Administration and Support.**
Principal's office, general office, parents' waiting area, sick room,
equipment store, sports kits room, service counter, and teachers' lounge.

**Sports and Recreation.**
Cricket ground, football ground, basketball court, courtyard badminton court
(21.5 m x 11 m), children's play area, and landscape gardens.


Campus Master Plan
------------------

The complete school layout provides clear zoning between academic,
administrative, sports, and service areas.  The campus is accessed via a
primary entry gate and exit gate along an access road.  Internal campus
roads are 7250 mm wide and connect all zones.

### Site Zoning

  * **Sports zone:** cricket ground (east), football ground (west),
    basketball court, and children's play area with perimeter fencing.
  * **Parking:** segregated zones for school bus parking, staff parking, and
    visitor parking to ensure safe drop-off and pick-up operations.
  * **Green areas:** landscape gardens, green buffers along campus roads, and
    fenced green areas for visual relief and environmental quality.
  * **Services:** site services area, plumbing pipe routes, and electrical
    ducts planned along circulation spines for maintainability.

### Circulation Flow

Entry gate -> bus and visitor parking -> administration block -> courtyard
and classrooms -> sports fields -> exit gate.

### Key Campus Dimensions

| ------------------------ | ------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Campus roads             | 7250 mm width       | Internal circulation     |

Ground Floor Design
-------------------

The ground floor hosts primary-level education (Nursery through Class VIII),
administrative functions, and the central courtyard.  It serves as the main
entry level with counter (+450 mm), office (+900 mm), and store (+900 mm)
level changes accommodating site topography.

| ------------------------- | -------------- | --------- | -------------------- |

| Office                    | 7.5 m x 7 m    | 53 m2     | Administrative staff |

  * 10-person lifts x 2, positioned at opposite wings for balanced access.
  * 14-person lift x 1, adjacent to admin and children's play zone.
  * Staircases at each wing terminus with UP/DN markers.
  * Plumbing and electrical ducts routed along corridor spines.

Structural and Material Considerations
--------------------------------------

### Foundation

  * Reinforced Concrete (RCC) for load-bearing capacity and earthquake
    resistance.
  * Piles or deep footings for weak soil conditions.
  * Steel Reinforced Concrete (SRC) for enhanced strength in critical zones.

### Walls

  * Brick masonry with cement mortar and plastering as the primary wall
    system.
  * Concrete blocks for improved thermal insulation.
  * Autoclaved Aerated Concrete (AAC) blocks for lightweight, fire-resistant
    construction.
  * Glass curtain walls on modern facade sections to maximize natural light.

### Roof

  * RCC slab with waterproofing.
  * Green roofs (vegetation on waterproof layers) for insulation and
    sustainability.
  * Polycarbonate panels in selected areas for natural light transmission.

### Flooring

  * Vitrified tiles for high-traffic corridors and classrooms.
  * Ceramic tiles for restrooms and laboratories.
  * Terrazzo flooring for corridors and public spaces.
  * Vinyl flooring for classrooms and play areas.
  * Rubber flooring for special needs classrooms and playgrounds.
  * Polished concrete for hallways and auditoriums.

### Doors and Windows

  * Aluminium frames with glass panels for natural light.
  * Wooden doors for classrooms and offices.
  * Steel doors at emergency exits for fire resistance.
  * UPVC windows for energy efficiency.
  * Tempered or laminated glass for safety glazing throughout.

### Structural Reinforcement

  * Steel beams and columns for G+2 frame support.
  * Reinforced concrete frames designed for seismic and wind loads.
  * Bracing systems (cross-bracing, shear walls) for earthquake resistance.
  * Fiber-Reinforced Polymer (FRP) reinforcements for supplemental support.

### Sustainability Integration

  * Solar panel provision for sustainable energy generation.
  * Cork boards and soft boards for classroom bulletin surfaces.
  * Fireproof and water-resistant paints on all interior surfaces.


Safety, Sustainability, and Accessibility
-----------------------------------------

**Emergency Exits and Fire Safety.**
Clearly marked fire exits and fire extinguishers on each floor.  Steel doors
at emergency egress points.  G+2 construction in fire-resisting materials
throughout.

**Disabled Accessibility.**
Ramps at the main entrance.  Three elevators (10-person x 2, 14-person x 1).
Wide doorways for wheelchair access throughout the building.

**Natural Ventilation and Lighting.**
Maximized through large windows, courtyard design, and doubly loaded corridor
typology.  Wide windows on the front elevation.

**Energy Efficiency.**
LED lighting throughout.  Extensive natural daylighting.  Provision for
rooftop solar panel installation.

**Water Management.**
Rainwater harvesting system and proper drainage planning.  Drinking water
stations on each floor wing.

**Green Campus.**
Landscape gardens, green area buffers, green roof option, and fenced
children's play zones for safe outdoor activity.


3D Visualization and Modeling
-----------------------------

The project team developed comprehensive 3D visualizations using Blender
4.3.2, translating 2D CAD floor plans into spatial models that validate
room proportions, circulation paths, and furniture layouts.

### Modeling Workflow

  1. Import 2D floor plan PDFs as reference images.
  2. Extrude walls from traced outlines.
  3. Build from modular mesh objects with Smooth-by-Angle modifiers (30
     degree threshold).
  4. Place desk-and-chair arrays in classrooms and lab benches in science
     rooms.
  5. Assemble ground, first, and second floors as separate collections.
  6. Integrate campus massing with landscaping via Sketchfab model import.

Design Notes and Constraints
----------------------------

  * The building uses doubly loaded corridors with a central courtyard at
    +300 mm elevation hosting an integrated badminton court.
  * Floor-to-floor height is 3600 mm across all three occupied levels.
  * Classroom standard size is 7 m x 7 m (49 m2) throughout all floors.
  * The library at 258 m2 is the largest single programmed space on campus.
  * Vertical zoning separates primary students (ground), secondary students
    (first), and specialized facilities (second) to minimize overlap between
    lab activities and younger student populations.
  * Three elevators and ramp access at the main entrance provide disabled
    accessibility throughout the G+2 structure.
